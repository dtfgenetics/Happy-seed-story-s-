## Purpose

This file defines the cover, title page, and visual identity direction for Book 1.

## Front Cover Goal

The cover should immediately communicate:
- warm children’s storybook
- magical Seed Valley world
- plant science adventure
- Benny as a lovable seed character
- germination happens underground
- friendship and curiosity

## Front Cover Scene

Benny Bean is tucked in soft soil, still mostly a seed. He looks hopeful, impatient, and a little worried. Penny Pea kneels nearby with her tiny notebook. Drip Water Drop glows gently in a small water path. Wally Worm peeks from a friendly tunnel. Sol the Sunbeam warms the soil from above.

The top of the cover may show a soft hint of the aboveground garden, but Benny should remain underground so the science topic is clear.

## Cover Composition

Foreground: Benny in soft soil, centered or slightly low-center.  
Left/side: Penny Pea observing.  
Near Benny: Drip Water Drop, gentle moisture.  
Lower/side tunnel: Wally Worm.  
Upper glow: Sol the Sunbeam.  
Background: soft soil textures, tiny roots, air spaces, warm aboveground light.

## Cover Mood

Cozy, magical, hopeful, premium, gentle, curious.

## Cover Text

Title:
Benny Bean Wakes Up

Series line:
A Happy Seed Stories Adventure

Optional tagline:
Every big plant begins with a tiny start.

## Do Not Show on Cover

- Benny as a full plant.
- Full mature bean vines.
- Flowering or fruiting stage.
- Harsh scientific diagram look.
- Flooded soil.
- Scary worm details.
- Too many unrelated Seed Valley characters.

## Title Page Direction

Title page should be calmer than the cover.

Scene option:
Seed Valley at sunrise with a cutaway view: aboveground warm garden glow, underground Benny sleeping as a closed seed in soft soil.

Text:
Benny Bean Wakes Up
A Happy Seed Stories / Seed Valley Adventure

## Dedication / Opening Page Visual Direction

Scene option:
A charming Seed Valley map with labeled or implied locations: Seed Nursery, Root Hollow, Worm Tunnel, Sunbeam Hill, Sprout Patch. Keep the map simple and magical, not overly detailed.

## Visual Continuity Rule

The cover should promise the same style and science sequence that the interior delivers: seed first, root first, shoot next, sprout last.
