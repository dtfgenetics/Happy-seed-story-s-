## Purpose

This file defines the first focused character style tests for Book 1. These are not final character approvals. They are production-ready test directions for choosing the approved visual look.

## Current Visual Stage

Visual Blueprint Built / Concept Art Phase Started.

## Test Goal

Create controlled visual tests for:

1. Benny Bean
2. Penny Pea
3. Drip Water Drop
4. Wally Worm
5. Sol the Sunbeam
6. Full cast lineup

Each character should be tested in three style variations: A, B, and C.

## Shared Test Format

Each character test sheet should include:

- front view
- 3/4 view
- side view if useful
- 4 emotion poses
- scale reference next to Benny
- notes on color, shape, and expression
- do-not-show notes

## Global Style Anchor

Warm premium early-reader children’s storybook illustration, charming rounded animated-style characters, expressive faces, soft natural lighting, colorful cozy Seed Valley plant-science world, gentle magical feeling, scientifically grounded, not hyper-realistic, not flat clipart.

## Benny Bean Style Test

### Benny A - Soft Classic Bean

Rounded tan bean seed body, simple friendly face, soft matte texture, tiny expressive arms or motion marks, very readable for ages 5 to 8. Best for classic picture book warmth.

### Benny B - More Animated Seed Hero

Slightly more expressive face, bigger eyes, subtle seed-coat texture, more dynamic poses, stronger cartoon personality while still premium and gentle.

### Benny C - Cozy Plush Seed

Softer plush-like bean form, extra rounded, warm cream/brown tones, cuddly emotional appeal, less science-detail texture but strong parent/child warmth.

### Benny Required Expressions

- impatient
- worried
- surprised by crack
- determined push
- proud tiny sprout

### Benny Do Not Show

No mature plant, no vines, no flowers, no bean pods, no full adult leaves in Book 1.

## Penny Pea Style Test

### Penny A - Gentle Observer

Small round green pea with calm face, tiny notebook, simple childlike proportions, thoughtful expression.

### Penny B - Curious Science Friend

Slightly more energetic design with notebook and pencil, expressive eyebrows, stronger observation role.

### Penny C - Soft Classroom Helper

Warm gentle pea character, softer colors, slightly larger notebook, designed to feel teacher-friendly and calm.

### Penny Required Expressions

- curious
- encouraging
- concerned
- delighted

### Penny Do Not Show

Do not make Penny visually dominate Benny in Book 1.

## Drip Water Drop Style Test

### Drip A - Cheerful Droplet

Simple blue water-drop character with friendly face, soft shine, rounded edges.

### Drip B - Bouncy Animated Drop

More expressive droplet with motion, playful posture, bright eyes, gentle glow.

### Drip C - Soft Magical Moisture Helper

More luminous, translucent, calm, gentle water presence, still friendly and kid-readable.

### Drip Required Expressions

- excited
- helpful
- amused
- gentle

### Drip Do Not Show

No flood, no aggressive splash, no drowning seed, no water-magic blast.

## Wally Worm Style Test

### Wally A - Friendly Soil Worm

Rounded soft worm with kind face, simple tunnel pose, warm pink/brown tone.

### Wally B - Funny Practical Worm

Slightly more animated expression, expressive eyebrows, small pointing gesture, comic timing.

### Wally C - Wise Soil Guide

Gentler elder-helper energy, calm smile, cozy tunnel setting, less goofy but still friendly.

### Wally Required Expressions

- amused
- wise
- helpful
- matter-of-fact

### Wally Do Not Show

No scary worm detail, no slimy gross realism, no teeth, no unsettling anatomy.

## Sol the Sunbeam Style Test

### Sol A - Gentle Sunbeam Face

Soft golden ray of light with kind face, subtle glow, warm expression above soil.

### Sol B - Warm Story Guide

More character-like sunbeam, expressive face, soft flowing ray shape, friendly storyteller energy.

### Sol C - Magical Light Presence

More atmospheric glow, less body, gentle face in golden light, premium magical tone without forcing growth.

### Sol Required Expressions

- patient
- wise
- encouraging
- welcoming

### Sol Do Not Show

No harsh beam, no laser light, no magical growth blast, no underground light forcing germination.

## Full Cast Lineup Test

Create one clean lineup of Benny, Penny, Drip, Wally, and Sol together on a neutral warm background.

Lineup requirements:

- Benny must be the visual lead.
- Penny should be smaller/supportive.
- Drip should feel light and playful.
- Wally should be friendly, not gross.
- Sol should read as warm helper, not a hard object.
- Scale should feel consistent and kid-friendly.

## Approval Questions

For each character, answer:

1. Is the character immediately lovable?
2. Is the science role clear?
3. Does the character match Seed Valley style?
4. Can the character show multiple emotions?
5. Will the design stay consistent across 32 pages?
6. Does anything look too generic, too mature, too realistic, or too childish?

## Recommended Next Action

Generate one character lineup style-test image with all five characters and three Benny variants. Use that to choose the approved visual direction before generating full page art.
