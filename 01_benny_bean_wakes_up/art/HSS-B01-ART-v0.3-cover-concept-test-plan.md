## Purpose

This file defines controlled front cover concept tests for Book 1. These are not final cover prompts. They are structured tests for choosing the approved cover direction.

## Cover Goal

The cover must sell three things quickly:

1. Benny is lovable.
2. The story is about germination and hidden growth.
3. Seed Valley feels warm, magical, premium, and educational.

## Required Cover Elements

- Benny Bean as a seed, not a mature plant.
- Soft underground soil setting.
- Hint of aboveground light/garden.
- Penny Pea, Drip Water Drop, Wally Worm, and Sol the Sunbeam present or implied.
- Clear title-safe space.
- Warm premium children’s storybook tone.

## Cover Concept A - Underground Cozy Close-Up

Benny is centered in soft soil, still mostly a closed seed, with Penny and Drip close by and Wally peeking from a tunnel. Sol’s warm glow enters from above.

Strength:
Most clearly communicates germination and emotional intimacy.

Risk:
May feel too underground if the top garden world is not hinted.

## Cover Concept B - Cutaway Seed Valley

A beautiful cutaway cover: aboveground garden sunrise at the top, underground Benny and friends below. Benny is the focus, but viewers can see that a bigger world waits above.

Strength:
Shows story world and science at once.

Risk:
Can become too busy if not controlled.

## Cover Concept C - Benny’s Big Question

Benny looks up from the soil with wide eyes asking “Is it time?” while friends gather around. More character-comedy focused, with fewer science diagram elements.

Strength:
Strong emotional hook and humor.

Risk:
May not communicate germination clearly enough unless soil/cutaway is strong.

## Cover Concept D - Tiny Sprout Promise

Benny is shown as a seed underground with a faint visual hint of his future sprout shape above him, like a soft dream or expectation.

Strength:
Shows the promise of growth.

Risk:
Must not imply he is already a mature plant or that growth is magical rather than biological.

## Cover Concept E - Classroom-Friendly Premium

Clean composition with Benny in soil, title space, soft diagram-like germination icons around the border, and the main cast placed gently around the scene.

Strength:
Best for teachers/classroom use.

Risk:
Could feel too educational if not storybook enough.

## Cover Test Requirements

Each cover test should be checked for:

- title readability
- Benny’s growth stage accuracy
- character consistency
- warmth and premium feel
- science clarity
- no visual overcrowding
- no mature plant imagery
- strong thumbnail readability

## Do-Not-Show Rules

Do not show:
- Benny as a full plant
- flowers or fruit pods on Benny
- root and shoot sequence incorrectly
- sunbeam magically forcing germination
- flooded soil
- scary worm details
- busy clutter behind title text

## Recommended First Test

Generate 3 cover concept tests first:

1. Concept A - Underground Cozy Close-Up
2. Concept B - Cutaway Seed Valley
3. Concept C - Benny’s Big Question

Choose the strongest direction, then refine it before creating final cover art.
