## Purpose

This file defines the first sample-page visual tests for Book 1. The goal is to prove the illustration style can handle emotion, science, underground scenes, aboveground scenes, back matter, and text-safe layout before generating all 32 page illustrations.

## Why Sample Pages Come First

Do not generate the full book page set until we know:

- Benny stays consistent across growth stages.
- The root-before-shoot science is visually clear.
- Underground pages are warm, not dark or scary.
- Aboveground reveal feels magical and earned.
- Text can fit safely on each page.
- Back matter can look educational without feeling like a worksheet.

## Required Sample Pages

## Sample 1 - Front Cover

Purpose: test market-facing visual identity.  
Must show: Benny underground, friends/support system, soft light, title space.  
Main risk: showing Benny too mature or making the cover too busy.

## Sample 2 - Page 3: “Is it time?”

Purpose: test Benny’s main personality and humor.  
Must show: closed seed Benny, impatient expression, quiet soil, optional sleepy pebble.  
Main risk: Benny not lovable enough or page feeling too plain.

## Sample 3 - Page 7: Drip and Swelling

Purpose: test moisture scene and early hidden change.  
Must show: Drip gently dampening soil, Benny swelling slightly, no crack yet.  
Main risk: flooding, water magic, or too much visible plant growth.

## Sample 4 - Page 16: First Root Appears

Purpose: test critical science reveal.  
Must show: tiny pale radicle emerging before shoot.  
Main risk: root too large, shoot appearing too soon, or incorrect sequence.

## Sample 5 - Page 21: Determination Push

Purpose: test emotional climax before emergence.  
Must show: Benny under heavy soil, root below, warm support from helpers, determination.  
Main risk: scene too dark, too stressful, or not readable.

## Sample 6 - Page 23: Tiny Sprout Emergence

Purpose: test climax and aboveground transition.  
Must show: Benny as tiny sprout, not mature plant; warm light and big garden scale.  
Main risk: sprout too mature or character identity lost.

## Sample 7 - Page 27: What We Learned

Purpose: test back matter visual style.  
Must show: simple germination stage sequence in correct order.  
Main risk: too textbook-like or inaccurate sequence.

## Sample 8 - Page 30: Observation Journal

Purpose: test printable classroom layout.  
Must show: drawing space, day lines, simple Benny/Penny icons.  
Main risk: too decorative, not enough blank space.

## Sample 9 - Page 32: Rooty Secret Teaser

Purpose: test series bridge into Book 2.  
Must show: Penny looking at Benny’s root path, notebook open, curiosity.  
Main risk: teaser too subtle or root path unclear.

## Approval Checklist for Each Sample

- Does the scene match the manuscript?
- Is the science accurate?
- Is Benny at the correct growth stage?
- Are characters consistent?
- Is text-safe space clear?
- Is the mood warm and premium?
- Is the page readable for ages 5 to 8?
- Does it avoid the do-not-show rules?

## Recommended Test Order

1. Character lineup.
2. Cover concept A/B/C.
3. Page 3 personality test.
4. Page 16 science test.
5. Page 23 climax test.
6. Back matter page test.
