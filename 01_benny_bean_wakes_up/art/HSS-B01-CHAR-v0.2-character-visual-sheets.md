## Purpose

This file gives detailed visual design guidance for Book 1 characters before final image prompts are created.

## Shared Style Rules

All characters should match the Seed Valley storybook style:
- warm premium early-reader children’s book look
- rounded friendly shapes
- soft natural lighting
- expressive faces
- gentle animated feel
- colorful but not harsh
- magical plant-science atmosphere
- clear silhouettes for young readers

## Benny Bean

Role: main character.  
Growth stages shown in Book 1: closed seed, slightly swollen seed, cracked seed, root emerging, root-down/shoot-up stage, tiny sprout.  
Base shape: plump bean seed, rounded oval, soft tan/brown/cream tones.  
Face: large expressive eyes, small mouth, highly readable emotions.  
Body language: wiggles, leans, puffs, stretches, huffs, pushes.  
Expression range: impatient, worried, surprised, determined, relieved, proud.  
Important continuity: Benny must remain visually recognizable as he changes. The sprout stage should still feel like Benny, not a new character.  
Do not show: mature bean plant, full vine, large leaves, flowers, fruit pods, or adult plant body in Book 1.

## Penny Pea

Role: observer friend and Book 2 bridge.  
Base shape: round pea character, green, gentle and curious.  
Props: tiny notebook or observation card; simple pencil optional.  
Face: thoughtful eyes, calm smile, curious eyebrow shapes.  
Body language: kneels, leans close, records observations, points gently.  
Expression range: curious, concerned, encouraging, delighted.  
Important continuity: Penny supports Benny and should not visually dominate Book 1.  
Do not show: Penny solving everything for Benny or becoming the main hero in Book 1.

## Drip Water Drop

Role: moisture helper.  
Base shape: round water-drop character with soft blue transparent glow.  
Face: cheerful, bright, playful.  
Body language: bouncing, floating, sliding through soil spaces, settling gently near Benny.  
Expression range: excited, helpful, amused, gentle.  
Science visual: moisture should dampen the soil without flooding it.  
Do not show: huge flood, drowning seed, water as magic spell, or hard splash impact.

## Wally Worm

Role: soil helper and gentle comic relief.  
Base shape: soft rounded worm, warm pink/brown tone, friendly face.  
Face: practical, amused, kind.  
Body language: peeking from tunnel, pointing out soil spaces, making room nearby.  
Expression range: amused, wise, helpful, matter-of-fact.  
Science visual: Wally can reveal soil tunnels and air spaces, but should not imply worms are the only reason soil has air spaces.  
Do not show: scary worm, slimy gross detail, or overly realistic anatomy.

## Sol the Sunbeam

Role: warmth and light helper.  
Base shape: soft golden sunbeam or glowing ray with kind face.  
Face: calm, warm, patient.  
Body language: glowing above the soil, shining softly, leaning down as a warm presence.  
Expression range: gentle, wise, encouraging.  
Science visual: Sol may warm the soil world; light becomes important after leaves emerge.  
Do not show: Sol blasting the seed, magically forcing germination, or shining underground as the main cause of sprouting.

## Environment Characters / Background Life

Allowed sparingly:
- sleepy pebble
- tiny soil crumbs with personality only if subtle
- background roots
- tiny air pockets
- small underground textures

Do not overcrowd the page. Book 1 should remain emotionally focused on Benny.

## Visual Consistency Checklist

Before approving any image:
- Can the child identify Benny immediately?
- Is Benny at the correct growth stage for the page?
- Is Penny supporting, not taking over?
- Is Drip gentle, not flooding?
- Is Wally friendly, not scary?
- Is Sol warm, not magical-forceful?
- Does the scene match the manuscript page purpose?
