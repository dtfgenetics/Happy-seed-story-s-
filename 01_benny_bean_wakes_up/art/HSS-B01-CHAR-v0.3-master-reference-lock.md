## Critical Production Distinction

Benny has two related but different visual applications:

### A. Series / Brand Character

- The exact Benny shown in the approved Seed Valley manifesto boards.
- Green bean character identity.
- Rounded, clearly bean-shaped silhouette.
- Recognizable worried-but-lovable face.
- Stable eye spacing, mouth shape, limb proportions, and color palette.
- This model represents Benny in brand boards, series materials, and later character-led scenes.

### B. Book 1 Seed-Stage Character

- A scientifically appropriate seed-stage version derived directly from the approved Benny model.
- Must preserve Benny’s approved face, silhouette language, palette relationship, eye spacing, and emotional identity.
- Must still read as Benny, not as a potato, egg, generic brown oval, or unrelated seed mascot.
- Pages 1–17: intact closed seed; no real root, crack, shoot, leaf, or sprout.
- Page 18: first small white/cream radicle appears.
- Page 20: first pale underground shoot appears.
- Page 23: first tiny above-ground sprout.
- End of Book 1: tiny seedling only; no mature bean plant.

Any decorative mascot sprout curl or mature plant feature must be removed from the closed seed-stage model when it would contradict the science sequence.

## Benny — Do Not Change

- Exact approved face and expression language.
- Clearly recognizable bean silhouette.
- Soft, friendly proportions.
- Small simple arms and feet.
- No glasses.
- No backpack.
- No flower accessory.
- No glossy plastic skin.
- No potato texture.
- No random freckles, seams, spots, or color changes unless present in the approved reference.

## Penny Pea — Do Not Change

- Round green pea character from the approved manifesto boards.
- Approved glasses and scarf remain part of her locked identity.
- Calm, observant, encouraging expression.
- She may carry a notebook or observation card when the scene calls for it.
- She remains a seed character in early Book 1 scenes and must not become a sprout.
- She supports Benny without visually taking over the story.

## Drip Water Drop

- Use the exact approved blue droplet model from the reference boards.
- Friendly, gentle, readable expression.
- Moisture helper, never a flood or magical spell.
- Same silhouette, face, highlights, and limb proportions on every appearance.

## Wally Worm

- Use the exact approved friendly rounded worm model.
- Clean, child-friendly, warm, and helpful.

- Same face and body proportions throughout.

## Sol the Sunbeam

- Use the exact approved warm sunbeam model.
- Gentle, encouraging, and softly luminous.

- No blasting rays, magical transformation beam, or harsh yellow filter.

## Model Sheet Requirement

Before page production resumes, create one approved visual sheet per character containing:

- front view;
- three-quarter view;
- side view;
- back view when useful;
- neutral pose;
- six core expressions;
- fixed palette swatches;
- height comparison;
- approved props;
- prohibited variations;
- Book 1 growth-stage variants for Benny.

## Layout Separation Rule

Character and environment illustrations must be created without generated story text.

The layout pass will add:

- exact manuscript wording;
- controlled line breaks;
- page number;
- cream rounded text panel;
- typography;
- print margins and bleed.

This separation is mandatory because generated text has repeatedly altered wording and introduced page errors.

## Approval Gate

A new illustration may proceed only when:

- the original approved reference image is available to the production tool during generation or editing;
- the correct character model sheet is also supplied;
- the page prompt names the exact growth stage;
- the image contains no generated text;
- the output is compared side-by-side against the approved reference before acceptance.
