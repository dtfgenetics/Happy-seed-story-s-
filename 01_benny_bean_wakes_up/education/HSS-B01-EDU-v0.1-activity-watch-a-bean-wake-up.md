## Science Topic

Germination.

## Science Question

What changes can we observe as a bean seed germinates?

## Materials

- dry bean seed
- clear cup or clear plastic bag
- paper towel
- water
- pencil or crayons
- observation page

## Steps

1. Dampen a paper towel. It should be damp, not dripping.
2. Place a bean seed against the paper towel.
3. Put the towel and seed in a clear cup or clear bag.
4. Keep the seed in a safe place where it can be observed.
5. Check the seed once a day.
6. Draw or write what changed.
7. Keep the towel damp if it begins to dry.

## Observation Targets

Look for:

- seed swelling
- seed coat softening or cracking
- first root
- shoot
- direction the root grows
- direction the shoot grows
- first tiny leaves later

## Expected Range of Results

Seeds may not all germinate at the same speed. Some may take longer. Some may not sprout if conditions are not right.

## Safety Notes

Adult supervision recommended. Do not eat experiment seeds. Wash hands after handling seeds, water, or plant materials. Throw away any seed or paper towel that becomes moldy.

## Parent / Teacher Note

This activity helps children practice patience, daily observation, sequencing, and drawing evidence.

## Journal Prompt

Draw your seed today. What changed since yesterday?
