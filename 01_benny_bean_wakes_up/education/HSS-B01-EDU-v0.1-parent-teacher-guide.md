## Purpose

This guide helps adults use Benny Bean Wakes Up as a story, science lesson, and observation activity.

## Read-Aloud Focus

Benny feels impatient because he cannot see himself growing yet. Ask children to notice how his feelings change as he learns that growth can happen in hidden stages.

## Discussion Questions

1. Why did Benny think nothing was happening?
2. What helped Benny begin changing?
3. What appeared first, the root or the shoot?
4. Why was Benny still growing even before he became a sprout?
5. When have you had to wait for something important?

## Science Clarification

Many seeds begin germination with moisture, oxygen, suitable temperature, and time. The first root often appears before the shoot. Light becomes especially important after leaves emerge.

Avoid saying all seeds need light to germinate.

## Activity Connection

Use the activity Watch a Bean Wake Up to help children observe seed changes over several days.

## Skills Practiced

- observation
- sequencing
- patience
- drawing evidence
- comparing daily changes
- science vocabulary

## Safety Notes

Adult supervision recommended. Do not eat experiment seeds. Wash hands after handling seeds, water, or plant materials. Throw away moldy materials.

## Extension Idea

Compare two seeds. Do they change on the same day? Draw both and compare what happens.
