## What We Learned

Benny Bean’s story shows how a seed can begin to grow before we see a sprout above the soil.

This beginning stage is called germination. Many seeds begin germination when they have moisture, oxygen, a suitable temperature, and time.

Water can help a seed swell and begin changing. The seed coat may soften or crack. In many common seeds, the first tiny root, called the radicle, appears before the shoot. The root grows downward, and the shoot grows upward.

After a shoot reaches the light and leaves begin to open, light becomes important for the young plant.

## Important Science Note

Not all seeds need the exact same conditions. Some seeds have special needs. That is why this book uses words like many, some, and often.

## Kid-Friendly Summary

A seed may look still, but when the conditions are right, tiny changes can begin inside. First the seed wakes up, then the root starts, then the shoot grows, and finally a sprout appears.

## Teacher Clarification

Avoid saying all seeds need light to germinate. Light is important for young plants after leaves emerge, but many seeds can begin germination underground without light.
