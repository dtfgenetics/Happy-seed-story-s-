## Draft-Safe Claims

The following claims are safe for the current working manuscript:

1. Germination is the beginning of seed growth into a seedling.
2. Many seeds begin germination when they have moisture/water, oxygen/air, suitable temperature, and time.
3. Water uptake can help a seed swell and can contribute to the seed coat splitting.
4. Seed requirements vary by species; some seeds have special light, darkness, dormancy, stratification, or scarification needs.
5. The radicle is the first root and often appears before the shoot in common germination descriptions.
6. Light is important for seedling growth after leaves emerge, but not all seeds require light to begin germination.

## Draft-Approved Language

Many seeds begin germination when they have moisture, oxygen, a suitable temperature, and time.

Water can help a seed swell and begin changing.

The seed coat may soften or crack.

In many common seeds, the first tiny root, called the radicle, appears before the shoot.

Light becomes important after leaves emerge.

Some seeds have special needs, and not all seeds germinate in the same way.

## Language Not Approved

All seeds need light to germinate.

Sunlight wakes every seed underground.

Every seed grows the same way.

The seed eats soil.

Roots drink like straws from puddles.
