## Current Supported Claims

The following claims are currently supported by the research pass and are safe as working-draft language:

1. Germination begins when a seed starts growing into a seedling.
2. Many seeds need moisture/water, oxygen/air, suitable temperature, and time.
3. Water uptake can help a seed swell and may contribute to seed coat splitting.
4. Germination conditions vary by species; some seeds have special dormancy or light/dark needs.
5. The radicle is the first root and often appears before the shoot in many common seed-germination descriptions.
6. Light is important for seedling growth after leaves emerge, but not all seeds require light to begin germination.

## Language Approved for Drafting

Many seeds begin germination when they have moisture, oxygen, suitable temperature, and time.

Water can help a seed swell and begin changing.

The seed coat may soften or crack.

In many common seeds, the first tiny root, called the radicle, appears before the shoot.

Light becomes important after leaves emerge.

Some seeds have special needs, and not all seeds germinate in the same way.

## Language Not Approved

All seeds need light to germinate.

Sunlight wakes every seed underground.

Every seed grows the same way.

The seed eats soil.

Roots drink like straws from puddles.
