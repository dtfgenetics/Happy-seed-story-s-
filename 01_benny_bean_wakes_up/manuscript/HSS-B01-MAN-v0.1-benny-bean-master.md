## Tagline

Every big plant begins with a tiny start.

## Core Refrain

Is it time?

## Science Lock

Many seeds begin germination when they have moisture, oxygen, suitable temperature, and time. Water helps the seed swell and begin changing. The seed coat softens or cracks. The first root, called the radicle, often appears before the shoot. The shoot grows upward. Light becomes important after leaves emerge.

## Science Claims to Avoid

- All seeds need light to germinate.
- Sunlight wakes every seed underground.
- Every seed grows the same way.
- Soil is food for the seed.
- Benny becomes a full plant by the end of Book 1.

## Core Cast

Benny Bean: impatient, funny, nervous, eager, dramatic.  
Penny Pea: curious observer and calm science friend.  
Drip Water Drop: cheerful water helper.  
Wally Worm: practical soil helper and gentle comic relief.  
Sol the Sunbeam: warm guide for warmth now and light later.

## Story Premise

Benny Bean wants to grow right now. But no matter how hard he wiggles, waits, and asks, he still looks like a seed. With help from Penny Pea, Drip Water Drop, Wally Worm, and Sol the Sunbeam, Benny discovers that growing can begin before anyone sees it.

## Beginning

Benny waits in the soft soil and worries that every other plant is growing except him.

## Middle

Drip brings moisture, Wally shows the hidden soil world, and Sol explains that warmth helps now while light matters later. Benny starts to feel changes inside even though he still cannot see them.

## Ending

Benny’s seed coat cracks, his first root grows down, his shoot grows up, and he finally breaks through as a tiny sprout. He realizes that starting small still counts. He comforts another seed and Penny begins wondering about roots.
