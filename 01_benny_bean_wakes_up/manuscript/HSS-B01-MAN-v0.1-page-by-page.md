## Purpose

This file holds the 32-page working structure for Book 1. It will become the locked page-by-page manuscript before illustration prompts are created.

## Page Structure

### Front Cover

Concept: Benny Bean in soft soil, curious and hopeful, with Penny Pea, Drip Water Drop, Wally Worm, and Sol the Sunbeam nearby.

### Page 1 - Title Page

Purpose: Introduce title and Seed Valley tone.

### Page 2 - Opening / Dedication Page

Purpose: Welcome readers into Seed Valley and the soft soil bed.

### Page 3

Beat: Benny waits underground and asks, “Is it time?”
Science purpose: A seed may look still before germination begins.
Emotional purpose: Introduce impatience.

### Page 4

Beat: Penny Pea gently observes Benny and encourages him to notice.
Science purpose: Observation matters.
Emotional purpose: Penny becomes calm support.

### Page 5

Beat: Benny compares himself to plants above and feels left behind.
Science purpose: Growth can happen at different times.
Emotional purpose: Make the problem clear.

### Page 6

Beat: Drip Water Drop arrives.
Science purpose: Moisture helps many seeds begin changing.
Emotional purpose: First helper arrives.

### Page 7

Beat: Benny feels himself swell slightly.
Science purpose: Water can help a seed swell and soften.
Emotional purpose: Hidden change begins.

### Page 8

Beat: Benny still cannot see anything and worries.
Science purpose: Germination begins out of sight.
Emotional purpose: Frustration rises.

### Page 9

Beat: Wally Worm opens a tunnel view of the soil world.
Science purpose: Soil has spaces where air and water can move.
Emotional purpose: Wonder and humor.

### Page 10

Beat: Penny notices that Benny is changing even if he cannot see it.
Science purpose: Observation reveals evidence.
Emotional purpose: Benny begins to trust small clues.

### Page 11

Beat: Sol the Sunbeam warms the soil above.
Science purpose: Suitable warmth helps many seeds begin.
Emotional purpose: Warm reassurance.

### Page 12

Beat: Sol explains warmth now, light later.
Science purpose: Avoid the false claim that all seeds need light to germinate.
Emotional purpose: Science clarification through story.

### Page 13

Beat: Benny feels a tiny pressure inside.
Science purpose: Internal growth is beginning.
Emotional purpose: Suspense.

### Page 14

Beat: Crack.
Science purpose: Seed coat starts to open.
Emotional purpose: Major page-turn moment.

### Page 15

Beat: Benny panics about the crack.
Science purpose: Seed coat opening is a normal stage.
Emotional purpose: Fear before understanding.

### Page 16

Beat: A tiny first root appears.
Science purpose: The radicle often appears before the shoot.
Emotional purpose: First visible progress.

### Page 17

Beat: The first root grows downward.
Science purpose: Roots often grow down and help anchor the plant.
Emotional purpose: Benny sees that down can be the right direction.

### Page 18

Beat: Benny wants to go up, but Penny explains the root has work to do first.
Science purpose: Root-before-shoot sequence.
Emotional purpose: Patience deepens.

### Page 19

Beat: The shoot begins pushing upward.
Science purpose: Shoot grows toward the surface.
Emotional purpose: Hope rises.

### Page 20

Beat: Soil feels heavy, but Benny keeps pushing.
Science purpose: Emergence takes effort and time.
Emotional purpose: Determination.

### Page 21

Beat: Wally clears space while Drip keeps things damp.
Science purpose: Roots, soil space, moisture, and air all matter.
Emotional purpose: Team support.

### Page 22

Beat: A tiny green curve nears the surface.
Science purpose: Shoot emergence.
Emotional purpose: Anticipation.

### Page 23

Beat: Benny breaks through as a tiny sprout.
Science purpose: Sprouting is early growth, not full maturity.
Emotional purpose: Climax.

### Page 24

Beat: Benny sees light for the first time.
Science purpose: Light becomes important after leaves emerge.
Emotional purpose: Wonder.

### Page 25

Beat: Benny realizes he is small but growing.
Science purpose: Growth continues after sprouting.
Emotional purpose: Acceptance.

### Page 26

Beat: Benny hears another seed asking, “Is it time?”
Science purpose: Seeds germinate at different times.
Emotional purpose: Benny becomes comforter.

### Page 27 - What We Learned

Topic: Germination basics.

### Page 28 - Words That Grew

Topic: seed, germination, seed coat, radicle, root, shoot, sprout, observe.

### Page 29 - Try It Yourself

Activity: Watch a Bean Wake Up.

### Page 30 - Observation Journal

Prompt: Draw what changes each day.

### Page 31 - Parent / Teacher Guide

Focus: patience, observation, hidden growth, safety notes.

### Page 32 - Next Book Teaser

Teaser: Penny wonders where Benny’s first root went and what roots do underground.

### Back Cover

Purpose: Sell story, science, emotional lesson, and classroom usefulness.
