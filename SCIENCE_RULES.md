## Purpose

This file protects the science accuracy of the series.

## Global Science Language Rule

Use careful words when science has exceptions:

- many
- some
- often
- may
- can
- suitable conditions

Avoid absolute claims unless they are clearly true.

## Book 1 Germination Lock

Approved wording:

- Many seeds begin germination when they have moisture, oxygen, suitable temperature, and time.
- Water helps the seed swell and begin changing.
- The seed coat softens or cracks.
- The first root, called the radicle, often appears before the shoot.
- The shoot grows upward.
- Light becomes important after leaves emerge.
- Some seeds have special requirements.

Forbidden or risky wording:

- All seeds need light to germinate.
- Sunlight wakes every seed underground.
- Every seed grows the same way.
- Soil is food for the seed.
- Roots drink like straws from puddles.
- Benny becomes a full plant by the end of Book 1.

## Visual Science Rule

Illustrations must not teach incorrect science. If an image is cute but scientifically misleading, it must be corrected.

## Research Rule

Science claims that are central to a book should be verified and logged in the Research Log before being marked Locked.
